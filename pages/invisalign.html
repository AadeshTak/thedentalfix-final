Start to Smile: Your Invisalign Timeline

Step 1

Digital Smile Scan

No goop, no mess. Just a quick, painless 3D scan of your teeth using advanced imaging. We'll even show you a preview of your future smile before you commit.

Step 2

Personalized Treatment Plan

Based on your scan, we'll create a custom plan. Every tiny movement is pre-planned using smart software, so your treatment is clear, efficient, and tailor-made.

Step 3

Receive Your Aligners

You'll get a set of clear aligners made just for you. Each set is worn for about 1–2 weeks. As you switch them, your teeth gradually shift into place, without any interruptions to your routine.

Step 4

Track Your Progress

We'll check in with you every 6–8 weeks. These are quick, simple visits, just to ensure everything's on track and you're happy with your progress.

Step 5

Maintain Your New Smile

Once your treatment is done, we'll provide retainers to help keep your smile in place. It's the final touch to a confident, lasting transformation.

Invisalign is a modern orthodontic treatment that uses a series of clear, removable aligners to straighten teeth without metal braces. It's discreet, comfortable, and custom-made for each patient.

Invisalign aligners gradually shift your teeth into the desired position by applying gentle, controlled force. You'll switch to a new set of aligners every 1–2 weeks as your treatment progresses.

Most patients experience mild pressure or discomfort during the first few days of wearing a new set of aligners, but it's generally much more comfortable than traditional braces.

The average treatment time with Invisalign ranges from 6 to 18 months, depending on the complexity of your case and how consistently you wear your aligners.

You should remove your Invisalign aligners before eating or drinking anything other than water. This prevents staining and damage to the aligners and keeps your mouth healthy.

Rinse your aligners daily, brush them gently with a soft toothbrush, and clean them with Invisalign cleaning crystals or mild soap. Avoid using hot water as it can warp the plastic.
